Positionality
Opacity Set Up

"One's social position or place in a given society in relation to race, ethnicity, and other statuses within systems of power and oppression. Positionality shapes a person's psychological experiences, worldview, and access to resources."

Keywords: social position, power structures, identity, reflexivity, privilege, situatedness

Stakes: When unconsidered, research lacks transparency and connection to current contexts, hindering reproducibility and reuse.

Silences
Opacity Discrimination Set Up Analyse

"A gap in the (historical) record resulting from the unintentional or purposeful absence or distortion of documentation." This applies to both documentation and institutional processes and policies.

Keywords: archival gaps, missing data, representation, absences, documentation

Stakes: Silences create skewed narratives where certain voices and experiences are not acknowledged or included.

Source Selection
Discrimination Opacity Collection

The systematic choices made about which sources, archives, or datasets to include or exclude from research, often reflecting institutional priorities, accessibility, and historical power structures.

Keywords: selection criteria, institutional bias, accessibility, power structures, archives

Stakes: Biased source selection can perpetuate existing inequalities and limit the scope of historical understanding.

Algorithmic Bias
Discrimination Process Analyse

Systematic and unfair discrimination that occurs when algorithms produce results that are prejudiced due to biased training data, flawed assumptions, or discriminatory design choices.

Keywords: machine learning, automation, discrimination, fairness, algorithms, AI

Stakes: Algorithmic bias can amplify and systematize discrimination, affecting decision-making at scale.

Representation
Discrimination Opacity Collection Analyse Share

How different groups, perspectives, or phenomena are depicted, included, or excluded in data and research, affecting whose voices are heard and whose experiences are documented.

Keywords: inclusion, diversity, voice, visibility, marginalization, demographics

Stakes: Poor representation can marginalize communities and create incomplete understandings of social phenomena.

Collaboration
Opacity Set Up Collection Share

The involvement of multiple stakeholders, communities, and perspectives in research processes, helping to address blind spots and ensure more inclusive and ethical research practices.

Keywords: stakeholder engagement, community involvement, plurivocality, participatory research

Stakes: Lack of collaboration can result in research that fails to serve the communities it studies.
